PRODUCT DESIGN
FLUIR

A simple product that converts a tap into a water fountain.

OVERVIEW

This was a solo project, part of the ‘Simple Product Design’ coursework during my second year at the National Institute of Design, India.

Fluir is a simple portable product that eliminates the use of water bottles and disposable glasses and can be used by people on the go or in their day to day lives.

ROLE AND DURATION

Research, prototyping, material exploration, product design

Solo Designer

1 month (Feb to Mar 2014)

THE CHALLENGE

The aim of this project was to identify a problem in our environment that can be solved with the help of a simple product solution. My project mainly focuses on how to improve the experience of drinking water from public drinking water taps and water coolers.

THE NEED
People often don’t carry or forget to carry a water bottle
Majority of schools, colleges and public spaces in India do not have water fountains
People use their hands to drink water from public drinking water taps without washing them first which is not hygenic.
Very few places have disposable glasses. Disposable glasses are not good for the environment.
Very few people actually buy a bottle of water.

PROBLEM AREAS

I spoke to people across different schools and colleges and in my locality, and majority of the people mentioned that they either drink water from public drinking water taps and water coolers using their bare hands on a daily basis or they have done it at least a few times. Many of them never carry a water bottle because they don’t prefer carrying one. Below are some of the problem areas they mentioned in the process of drinking water from water coolers.

INITIAL CONCEPTS

Water tap attachments
Collapsibility

THE FINAL CONCEPT

The final concept is a simple, portable water tap attachment, that the user can carry with himself and attach to the water tap to convert the water tap into a fountain and drink water with ease, without using his hands.

PROTOTYPING

1
The final product was first made in thermocole and PU foam to test out various forms and dimensions.
2
Next, it was made in plastic to see how the internal shape should be to pass the right amount of water with the right amount of force.
3
Finally, the end product was casted in silicone rubber with the help of a positive and negative mold and an internal core.

TARGET AUDIENCE

People who find carrying a bottle too cumbersome
People who want to drink water from public water taps while travelling
People using watercoolers in schools and colleges

KEY TAKEAWAYS

1.  I explored a lot of different materials and media while prototyping starting from paper prototyping, paper models, thermocole models, PU foam models, mold making, silicone model to 3D renders.

2.  This was the first actual working product that I built and a major learning was that no matter how simple the product, there is still a lot of thought behind how it functions.

3.  Learning from failures. A lot of my prototypes failed during the initial exploration phase. However, I kept at it and finally had a working prototype.
