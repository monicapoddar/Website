OPEN SOURCE SERVICE DESIGN
DEAR GREEN PLACE

An open source service design project on urban community gardening for ‘Future Cities Glasgow’ and the ‘Glasgow City Council’.

OVERVIEW

This open source live project was a part of the coursework during my exchange semester in the product design department at The Glasgow School of art, Scotland, UK. I worked in a team of 5 designers from around the world.

The project mainly focuses on how urban gardening can help tackle the problem of mental health and unemployment in the Future City of Glasgow.

ROLE AND DURATION

Research, Service Design, Business Strategy

5 Designers from India, France, Portugal and Glasgow

1 month (Nov to Dec 2014)

ABOUT THE CLIENT

Future Cities Glasgow is an innovation programme run in partnership between the Technology Strategy Board and the City of Glasgow. Its aim is to demonstrate what a future city looks like - prototyping new concepts in urban design, citizen engagement and infrastructure to understand how technology impacts them, so that the findings can be fed back to the TSB and local governments across the UK.

Globally, the Future Cities Market is estimated to be over $1 Trillion USD by 2020, and, within that, the UK aims to be a leading provider of products and services to that global market. In this context - the Future Cities programme is a significant research effort into how this may be achieved. Open Glasgow is the citizen engagement portion of the programme. It operates an open data portal, where information about the city is collected, processed and published for citizens to use to gain insight into their city.

THE CHALLENGE

The goal of this project was to come up with a design solution in the form of a service for the healthcare sector for the ‘Glasgow City Council’ and ‘Future Cities Glasgow’. Each group was posed with some imminent questions and a part of the project was also to question and try and tackle some of these wicked problems.

“How can we bridge the gap between citizen needs and the local government?”
“How can we solve the problem of unemployment in the Future City of Glasgow?”

We also made a video to validate the issue and highlight how urban gardening could be a viable solution to tackle the problem of mental health and unemployment in the city of Glasgow.

WHAT HAPPENED NEXT ?

We presented this idea in an exhibition that was set up by ‘Future Cities Glasgow’ and the interest was phenomenal.

WHAT THE PARTICIPANTS
HAD TO SAY?
When and where can we start?
How can we engage the citizens?
What are the different sources of revenue generation?

THE GLASGOW SERVICE MAP

Post the exhibition and the session we also proposed a service map and a Business Strategy to the Glagow City Council on how it could begin from the existing community gardens and grow from there, making the citizens self sufficient and healthy as well as generate employment. This also demonstrates the different sources of revenue generation.

KEY TAKEAWAYS

1.  I explored the application of the design process within a social and economic context to gain a better understanding about the network of social and organisational relationships that frame user-experience.

2.  I learnt to visualise and process-map complex problems and issues as a means of identifying product, service and experience design opportunities and utilise design thinking and action as a tool for cultural and organisational change.

3.  There is a lot of open source data out there that can be easily accessed to create viable design solutions.

4.  Each designer in the team was from a different country and brought a different social aspect to the project.
