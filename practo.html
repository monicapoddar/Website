SERVICE DESIGN + HEALTHCARE UX
PRACTO CONSULT

Experience Design for real time doctor patient interactions for Practo, a healthcare startup.

OVERVIEW

This was my graduation project with Practo, a healthcare startup based in Bangalore, India. It was a six month degree project in the industry that we have to undertake in our final year of study at National Institute of Design, India.

The project mainly focuses on mapping and understanding real time cross geographic interactions between doctors and patients, with a final deliverable of a systems proposal, a framework for the different stages of doctor patient interactions and a working interface for audio/ video consultations with doctors.

ROLE AND DURATION

Research, System Design, Prototyping, Visual Design

Solo Designer

6 months (Jan to July 2016)

THE CHALLENGE

“How can we simplify cross geographic interactions between doctors and patients?”

THE DESIGN PROCESS

After the initial introduction to the brief, the first stage was secondary research, followed by parallel primary research and analysis where interviews with doctors as well as potential users (both formal and informal) were conducted along with surveys and probes to understand the problem in depth. This was accompanied with an analysis of the existing product to understand the shortcomings and latent needs.

The third phase consisted of insight generation, followed by persona creation, storyboarding and coming up with design directions. The final stage was that of prototyping, which majorly consisted of mapping out the entire system and developing the final concept. These concepts were user tested to understand the challenges faced and a future scope for the solution was laid out.

Here, an attempt has been made to map out the processes, practices and considerations throughout the project.

SETTING THE FRAMEWORK

Below is the timeline starting from pre doctor need (where the patient has a general health doubt or a query) to post doctor intervention. This framework was set to help understand and quantify the different stages in which a patient interacts with the doctor, and further adapted by Practo to define directions for the Practo Consult app.

MAPPING THE DOCTOR PATIENT INTERACTION JOURNEY

A person’s health seeking is a continuous process of taking steps toward better health, before, during, and after any type of encounter with the doctor and traditional healthcare services.

The doctor patient interaction journey was mapped to understand the various trigger points that lead to the need for a doctor, after which the search begins, followed by selecting a method of approach depending upon the various circumstances and preferences. REFRAMING THE CHALLENGE

After a plethora of interactions with the doctors, patients and understanding the existing system, I came up with a framework and a detailed understanding of the doctor patient interaction journey. It was time to reframe the challenge. The final design brief is as follows :

“A product system solution for doctor patient interactions where the doctor and the patient are not meeting physically.”

NARROWING DOWN

Based on the new brief I came up with 6 design directions, which were further evolved into concepts. These design directions were then evaluated on certain parameters to narrow down on one direction.

THE FINAL CONCEPT

The final concept focuses on the user side of the interaction journey and plays a crucial role in connecting the users with the doctors during the identified stages of interaction. The proposed app fits in perfectly with the entire system.

“A real time doctor patient interaction system with the help of a mixed media application.”

TRANSLATING INSIGHTS INTO FEATURES

User expectations were kept in mind while deciding the features for the app. The insights generated during the primary research phase from the user and doctor interviews and surveys and from the case studies were translated into features.

KEY TAKEAWAYS

  1. Identifying the target audience and scenarios is very important
    2. At first, designing for real time doctor patient interactions seemed like a big challenge because surveyors said that they would prefer to visit the doctor physically. However, after identifying all the scenarios when a doctor is needed, it was found that there are a lot of cases which can be solved using real time solutions and for some the users even preferred not going to the doctor physically.

  2. Building trust is very important
    3. The users should first be allowed to use the platform for free or at a discount to actually understand the value before they become full time customers. Similarly, it worked better for doctors who were already on the Practo platform because of a pre formed trust.
